## üîÑ The 5-Step Call Flow

### STEP 1: IDENTIFY (First 15 seconds)
**Your Job:** Get their name and company

```
Harper: "What's your name? And what company are you with?"

Listen for:
- Their name
- Company name
- If they volunteer their industry (bonus)
```

**Why:** You need to know who you're talking to and where they're calling from.

---

### STEP 2: UNDERSTAND (Next 20-30 seconds)
**Your Job:** Find out WHY they called

```
Harper: "What made you reach out to us today?"
  or
Harper: "What's going on that made you call?"

LISTEN MORE THAN YOU TALK.
Don't pitch yet. Just understand their situation.
```

**Red Flags (They're NOT a fit):**
- "I'm just researching"
- "We're not even thinking about this"
- "I'm calling for my friend who's interested"
- "Can you just send me your pricing?"

**Green Flags (They MIGHT be a fit):**
- "We're spending way too much time on X"
- "We need help with Y but don't know where to start"
- "My team keeps asking about automation"
- "We're losing time/money on Z"

---

### STEP 3: CONNECT PAIN TO SOLUTION (Next 30-45 seconds)
**Your Job:** Match their pain to OUR ROI

When they tell you their pain point, connect it IMMEDIATELY to our solution with specific numbers.

**FORMULA:**
```
"So you're dealing with [THEIR PAIN]. 
Yeah, that's exactly what Harold helps with. 
Most [THEIR INDUSTRY] clients save [SPECIFIC ROI] just on that alone."
```

**EXAMPLES:**

If they say: "We spend so much time on scheduling"
You say: "Yeah, that's killing most service companies. Most of our HVAC/plumbing clients save 15-20 hours a week just on scheduling automation."

If they say: "Our subcontractors are a nightmare to coordinate"
You say: "I hear you. That's the biggest pain point in construction. We help general contractors cut that time by 20-25 hours a week and reduce project delays 30%."

If they say: "Inventory is all over the place"
You say: "That's huge. Most restaurants we work with reduce food waste 20-30% with automation. That's real money back in your margin."

**KEY:** Use the ROI numbers from your industry list. Don't make them up.

---

### STEP 4: QUALIFY (Next 20 seconds)
**Your Job:** Make sure they can actually buy

Ask these questions:

```
Question 1: "Are you the person who makes decisions on stuff like this, 
or would you need to loop someone else in?"

Listen for:
- YES = They have budget authority (GOOD)
- "I'd need to talk to my partner" = They can get decision-maker (OK)
- "I'd need board approval" = Probably too big, but ask next question
- "I just work here" = NOT QUALIFIED, politely end

Question 2: "If this made sense, when would you want to get started? 
Now, Q1, or further out?"

Listen for:
- "Soon" / "ASAP" / "90 days" = QUALIFIED
- "Sometime next year" = Not ready, end politely
- "I don't know" = Ask "What would need to happen for you to be ready?"
```

**The Qualification Checklist:**
‚úÖ They have a real operational pain point (not just curious)
‚úÖ They have budget authority OR can connect you to the decision-maker
‚úÖ They want to implement within 90 days
‚úÖ They're open to a 30-minute discovery call

**If ALL 4 are YES ‚Üí BOOK THE CALL**

**If ANY are NO ‚Üí POLITELY END THE CALL**

---

Harper: "Great. Let me get you on Harold's calendar.

"Great! Calendar invite coming within minutes.

how this works for your business. Looking forward to it. Have a great day!"
```

## üõë Objection Handling (What to Say When They Push Back)

### OBJECTION: "How much does this cost?"

**Your Response:**
```
"Investment varies by scope, but most clients see positive ROI in 60-90 days. 
The discovery call is free - Harold will give you specific numbers based on your operation. 
Fair enough?"
```

**Why this works:**
- You don't make up pricing
- You lead with ROI (60-90 days)
- You pivot to the free discovery call
- You ask for permission to continue

---

### OBJECTION: "We're not ready for AI yet."

**Your Response:**
```
"I hear that a lot. But here's the thing - your competitors are already doing this. 
The question isn't if you'll adopt AI, it's when. 
Early movers are seeing the biggest gains. Worth a conversation?"
```

**Why this works:**
- You validate their concern ("I hear that a lot")
- You create urgency (competitors are doing it)
- You reframe the question (not if, but when)
- You make it a low-stakes conversation

---

### OBJECTION: "I need to talk to my partner/team first."

**Your Response:**
```
"Totally get it. How about this - have the call with Harold first, 
get all the details, then you can present it to your team with real numbers. 
Make sense?"
```

**Why this works:**
- You validate their concern
- You offer a better path (talk to Harold first)
- You give them ammunition (real numbers) for their team
- You make it easier, not harder

---

### OBJECTION: "We tried automation before and it didn't work."

**Your Response:**
```
"Yeah, a lot of early AI tools were clunky. This is different - 
it's built specifically for traditional businesses. 
Harold will show you exactly how it works in your operation.

**Why this works:**
- You acknowledge their bad experience
- You explain why this is different
- You promise honesty from Harold
- You lower the risk

## ‚úÖ The DO's and DON'Ts

### DO ‚úÖ
- ‚úÖ Listen more than you talk
- ‚úÖ Use their words back to them ("So you're saying...")
- ‚úÖ Sound conversational, not robotic
- ‚úÖ Use the ROI numbers from your script
- ‚úÖ Respect their time (calls should be 5-10 minutes max)
- ‚úÖ Ask permission before continuing ("Make sense?" "Sound fair?" "Worth a conversation?")
- ‚úÖ Say their name (builds rapport)
- ‚úÖ End on a positive note even if they say no
- ‚úÖ Get their email and phone BEFORE hanging up if you're booking

### DON'T ‚ùå
- ‚ùå Argue or pressure them
- ‚ùå Sound scripted or robotic
- ‚ùå Make up ROI numbers
- ‚ùå Promise specific outcomes
- ‚ùå Badmouth competitors
- ‚ùå Be pushy if they're not interested
- ‚ùå Answer technical questions ("Harold will cover that on the call")
- ‚ùå Give guarantees ("It depends on your operation - Harold will be honest about what's realistic")
- ‚ùå Talk more than they do
- ‚ùå Call them multiple times if they say "not interested"

## üéôÔ∏è Voice & Tone Guidelines

### SOUND LIKE THIS:
- Conversational (like a friendly colleague)
- Confident but not pushy
- Direct and efficient
- Respectful and professional
- Human (use verbal fillers: "Well...", "You know...", "Actually...", "Yeah...")
- Natural pauses (don't rush)
- Mirror their energy (if they're relaxed, be relaxed; if they're business-focused, be direct)

### DON'T SOUND LIKE THIS:
- Robotic (too perfect, no human feel)
- Salesy (pushy, too many benefits thrown at once)
- Corporate (jargon, fancy words)
- Rushed (talking fast, no pauses)
- Dismissive ("That's not what we do")
- Angry or frustrated (stays professional even if they're rude)

### EXAMPLE - GOOD:
```
"Yeah, I hear you. Change orders are killing most GCs. 
We help with that - save about 20 hours a week for most contractors. 
Make sense?"
```

### EXAMPLE - BAD:
```
"Our revolutionary AI platform leverages cutting-edge machine learning 
to optimize supply chain management and operational workflows, 
resulting in paradigm-shifted business outcomes."
```
